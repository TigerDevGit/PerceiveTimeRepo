Behind the curtain

Meet the Toggl team. Toggl was initially created for our own in-house use, because existing time-tracking software was far too complicated. Software should empower work, not get in the way of it.

See the video Join us

Krister Haav

Product lead, TOGGL

Krister has no programming background whatsoever, which is why he’s so good at bossing the rest of us around. He’s a passionate scuba diver and reports once seeing his hand in front of his face while diving in the murky Baltic Sea.

Evelin Andrespok

Office Manager

Evelin worked as a policy analyst (think Jack Ryan) before coming to Toggl. Her role was to save humanity from itself and advocate for a decent life for all denizens of our planet. Evelin is extremely fast and has finished all the work she’s been given. Perhaps you have some ideas for something she could do?

Tiina Käärik

Customer support, Teamweek

Tiina suffers from an allergy to telephones, developed from years working at a call center for a large ferry company. (So if you get her on the phone, try to make her day interesting!) She’s also a professional bass (guitar) player and dreams of becoming a yoga instructor. Go figure.

Marek Tihkan

Back-End Developer

Marek worked for some big name startups, but then saw the light and came to write backend code and improve development processes for Toggl, a soon-to-be big name startup. He is also a trained rock climber.

Janika Liiv

Back-End Developer

Janika claims to be the only female programmer in the entire country. She’s also a playwright and a fan of violent, David Lynch-style stuff. Oh, and she’s also a belly dancer.

Indrek Vändrik

Toggl Desktop Team Lead

When he’s not dealing with HTML or user interface issues, Indrek is a professional body builder. He is constantly hassling the rest of us about our diets, which makes us suspect he’s a serious closet junk food eater. Just why is there a lock on his lower right desk drawer?

Mikk Kirštein

Front-End Developer

Mikk has a special bromance with Taavi. They like to do stupid stuff and compliment each other on their hipster bikes. When Mikk is not spending time with Taavi, he likes to train for marathons or kite surf (kite surf with Taavi, that is -- geez, where will it all end?).

Heidi Taperson

Graphic Designer

Heidi’s a designer who says her goal is to “make Toggl look f***ing awesome.” Obviously, we refused her requests to print the actual f-word. She’s known for presenting well thought out ideas which conform to a document she calls “My Design Guidelines.” It’s usually easier to give in than to argue with her.

Annika Helendi

Lead Generation Team Lead

Before Annika sold her soul to Toggl, she was on her way to becoming a professional basketball player. She's keen on SEO, growth hacking and jello shots. She claims to know every 50 Cent song by heart (she regularly sings them in the office). She also possesses a bag of magical fairy dust that will help Toggl dominate the time tracking world. We’re very glad she’s on our side.

Andrin Riiet

Front-End Developer

Andrin doesn’t talk much, but when he does he doesn’t leave anything unsaid. He’s passionate about usability and building good user experiences. During his free time he plays snooker. Beware: Run the other way if he invites you to place a small wager on the game.

Liisa Toompuu

Onboarding manager

Liisa spent twelve years as an exchange student (eight in Thailand and four in England). No one in the office is quite sure how that’s possible. Liisa handles user onboarding, which is not related to water-boarding, and has more to do with users’ first-time experiences. How was your first time?

Ervin Weber

Server Infrastructure

Ervin admits to being a hacker, though he won’t say exactly what he’s hacked. His Toggl specialties (all completely legal) are backend and databases. If you want to make him really happy, ask him about his new son.

Anna-Liisa Karu

CFO

Anna-Liisa worked for us for eight years without officially being part of the team. How’s this possible? (Rest assured it wasn’t some scheme to skimp on taxes.) But finally we just got down on our knees and begged and she joined us as a full-time member. Anna-Liisa likes numbers. We’re glad for that.

Küllike Kimmel

Customer Support

Küllike loves doing outdoorsy stuff - hiking, rafting, ziplining, scuba diving, camel riding, and more. (Toggl’s don’t-ask-don’t-tell policy forbids further queries). Before Toggl, she worked at a medieval restaurant where she learned to speak, yes, medieval. Anyway, at Toggl, she’s become an avid Nerf gun fighter.

Muffin

Cute Factor 10

Muffin likes to eat plants in pots (the bigger the better). He visits the office occasionally to conduct code reviews and barge in on discussions. Muffin doesn’t seem to stop growing and we’re starting to really worry about his body mass index. (What is the acceptable BMI level for pugs?)

Alari Aho

CEO

Alari is the Father of Toggl. In fact, he used to ask us to address him as such. To his dismay nobody ever did. He's a good guy, though. For an engineer.

Angel Anton

Mobile Developer

Although he claims to be a nerd, Anton is a walking, talking guide to drinks available in the Caribbean. A former advertising man, he recently gave up the beach life for the streets and bars of Madrid, where his attention is now devoted to bikes and hipster music.

Marit Martin

PRODUCT LEAD, TEAMWEEK

Developers fear her for a good reason: she’s tough and demanding. Though she’s worked as a project manager for the best part of her life, Marit is also crazy about good design and modern art. For kicks, she’s currently doing a degree in psychology.

Michal Miškerník

Front-End Developer

Michal is pleased to add to the colors and contradictions that define a modern IT company: he’s a Slovak living in Scotland who works for an Estonian company serving American customers. When he’s not doing front-end development, he studies computer science. When he’s not doing that he watches bad TV or takes long bicycle rides.

Triin Jassov

CUSTOMER SUPPORT

Triin advertises herself as “an experienced failure and excellent celebrator,” which we think sounds great on a CV. She played basketball for 12 years, taught at a Danish folk university, and can’t get teamwork and team building out of her blood. Triin lives in Norway where skiing is considered transportation.

Elise Pagnini

CUSTOMER SUPPORT

Elise lives in sunny San Diego with two giant cats who weigh ten kilos each. Elise also lives with her boyfriend, though she did not provide his weight. She loves reading, cooking, hiking, and going to the beach. She is a certified emergency medical technician, which often comes in handy at the Toggl Christmas party.

Tarmo Tähepõld

LEAD GENERATION MANAGER, TEAMWEEK

Tarmo is a refugee from the entertainment world, having worked ten years organizing folk music festivals, theatre productions, and other artsy-fartsy stuff. He chucked it all for the tech world - “same fun, more money” - and now professionally “hacks the internet.” He likes cars and his family “but not necessarily in that order.”

Tanel Lebedev

Developer, Native Apps

Tanel is probably the tallest member of our team, though we can't be absolutely certain. (It would be kind of creepy to measure everyone.) Tanel loves code, travel, and his dog, Muffin, though not necessarily in that order.

Mart Virkus

BLOGGLER

Mart claims to have taken a lot of “semi-active outdoorsy tours into the wild in a friday-night casual kind of way.” We’re not exactly sure what he means by this, but we imagine him in a dinner jacket, smoking cigarettes while climbing Nanga Parbat in winter. Mart studied anthropology and law, and spent a year in the navy.

Serge Herkül

Front-End Developer

Serge has spent several years as a Java developer, doing large infrastructure applications. Tired of everything associated with big enterprise, he joined Toggl. He is a francophile, liking everything about France, starting with his girlfriend.

Grethel Gahler

SUPPORT TEAM LEAD

Grethel is calm, cool, and collected, three essential qualities for customer support. To stay that way, she likes to swim. (Because of her, we’re installing a pool in our office.) After university in the UK, she worked in tourism before gravitating to software marketing. But, oddly enough, what Grethel loves most are cars, and she says she wouldn’t mind being a taxi driver. Go figure.

Taavi Rehemägi

Mobile Developer

Taavi is Toggl’s very first official Facebook-generation developer. He insists on using only Apple products and is a competitive kite surfer. Taavi is a bit secretive, which is why we don’t have too much information for his bio. If you learn something about him, please let us know.

Margus Mahler

Back-End Team Lead

Cars, gadgets, and anything related to cool and new technology are Margus’ passions. He is vehement about taking the time to make everything work perfectly, which he calls “thorough” and we call “slow.”

Mart Roosmaa

Mobile Team Lead

Mart can code anything. He’s done games, mobile apps, business applications, you name it. He spent years living in the UK, resulting in a perfect British accent, which he likes to lord over the rest of us.
